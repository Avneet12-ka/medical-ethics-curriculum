Case Study 10: Research Ethics Application

Introduction

Introduction

Welcome to Case Study 10: Research Ethics Application. In this interactive case study, you will explore ethical challenges in medical research, particularly focusing on research involving vulnerable populations in Malaysia.

You will follow Dr. Aisha Binti Rahman, a neurologist conducting research on early Alzheimer's disease, as she navigates the complex ethical landscape of research ethics committee approval, informed consent, protocol adherence, and publication ethics.

Throughout this case study, you will be presented with ethical dilemmas and asked to make decisions. Your choices will help you understand the practical application of research ethics principles in the Malaysian context.

Key Concepts in Research Ethics

Fundamental Ethical Principles
  • Respect for Persons: Recognizing autonomy and protecting those with diminished autonomy
  • Beneficence: Maximizing benefits and minimizing harms
  • Justice: Fair distribution of research benefits and burdens
  • Non-maleficence: Avoiding harm to research participants
Informed Consent Process
  • Elements of valid consent: disclosure, understanding, voluntariness, capacity
  • Special considerations for vulnerable populations
  • Ongoing consent and the right to withdraw
  • Documentation requirements
Research Ethics Committee Structure and Function
  • Composition and expertise requirements
  • Review procedures and decision-making
  • Continuing review and monitoring
  • Reporting requirements
Protocol Adherence and Deviations
  • Types of protocol deviations
  • Reporting requirements
  • Corrective and preventive actions
  • Impact on data integrity
Incidental Findings Management
  • Categories of incidental findings
  • Disclosure obligations
  • Participant preferences
  • Follow-up procedures
Publication Ethics
  • Complete and transparent reporting
  • Authorship criteria
  • Conflicts of interest
  • Data sharing responsibilities
Community Engagement in Research
  • Benefits of community involvement
  • Methods for meaningful engagement
  • Addressing diversity and inclusion
  • Returning results to communities

Scene 1: The Research Proposal

Neuroscience Research Laboratory

Neuroscience Research Laboratory, University Malaya Medical Centre

Dr. Aisha:

"This new treatment approach could significantly slow cognitive decline in early Alzheimer's patients. I'm confident our research will make a breakthrough."

Dr. Aisha:

"The preliminary data from our animal studies is promising. I believe we're ready to move to human trials."

Dr. Raj:

"The data does look promising, but human trials will require rigorous ethical review. Have you considered all the ethical implications?"

Dr. Aisha:

"Of course. The benefits far outweigh the risks. This could help thousands of patients if successful."

Dr. Raj:

"I'm particularly concerned about your inclusion criteria. You're targeting patients with early Alzheimer's who may have fluctuating capacity to consent."

Dr. Aisha:

"We'll implement a thorough consent process. This population desperately needs new treatment options."

Dr. Raj:

"I agree about the need, but we must ensure our process protects vulnerable participants. Let's review the consent procedure together."
