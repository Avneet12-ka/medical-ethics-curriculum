Case Study 9: Interprofessional Conflict

Introduction

Introduction

Welcome to Case Study 9: Interprofessional Conflict. In this interactive case study, you will explore the ethical dimensions of professional relationships and conflict resolution in Malaysian healthcare settings.

You will follow a team of healthcare professionals at Hospital Kuala Lumpur as they navigate complex interprofessional dynamics, communication challenges, and ethical dilemmas that arise when team members disagree about patient care.

Throughout this case study, you will be presented with ethical dilemmas and asked to make decisions. Your choices will help you understand the practical application of professional ethics principles in the Malaysian healthcare context.

Key Concepts in Professional Relationships

Ethical Principles in Collegial Relationships
  • Respect for Colleagues: Recognizing the dignity and value of all healthcare team members
  • Integrity: Maintaining honesty and trustworthiness in professional communications
  • Justice: Treating colleagues fairly and equitably
  • Beneficence: Working together for patient benefit
  • Fidelity: Maintaining loyalty to professional commitments
  • Solidarity: Supporting colleagues in difficult circumstances
Professional Codes and Guidelines
  • Malaysian Medical Council Code of Professional Conduct: Sections on relationships with colleagues
  • Malaysian Nursing Board Code of Professional Conduct: Guidelines for nurse-physician relationships
  • Allied Health Professions Act Regulations: Standards for interprofessional interactions
  • Hospital Bylaws and Policies: Institutional rules governing professional relationships
Cultural Dimensions of Professional Relationships
  • Hierarchical Structures: Traditional respect for seniority and authority
  • Collectivist Values: Emphasis on group harmony over individual assertion
  • Indirect Communication: Preference for non-confrontational approaches
  • Face-Saving Practices: Avoiding public criticism or disagreement
  • Ethnic and Religious Diversity: Varying cultural norms across Malaysia's multicultural society
Interprofessional Collaboration Models
  • Team-Based Care: Coordinated approach involving multiple disciplines
  • Shared Decision-Making: Collaborative determination of care plans
  • Role Clarity: Understanding scope of practice and responsibilities
  • Mutual Respect: Valuing diverse professional contributions
  • Information Sharing: Effective communication across disciplines
Conflict Resolution Strategies
  • Direct Communication: Clear, respectful expression of concerns
  • Active Listening: Attentive engagement with others' perspectives
  • Interest-Based Problem Solving: Focusing on underlying needs rather than positions
  • Mediation: Third-party facilitation of difficult conversations
  • Escalation Pathways: Appropriate channels for unresolved conflicts

Scene 1: The Ward Round Disagreement

Medical Ward, Hospital Kuala Lumpur

Medical Ward, Hospital Kuala Lumpur, Morning Ward Round

Dr. Ahmad:

"Encik Tan's condition has stabilized, but he still requires close monitoring. We'll continue with the current treatment plan."

Dr. Kumar:

"With respect, Dr. Ahmad, I've been reviewing the latest guidelines. There's a newer treatment approach that might be more effective for his condition."

Dr. Ahmad:

"I'm familiar with those guidelines, Dr. Kumar. In my experience, the traditional approach works well for our patient population. We'll continue as planned."

Sister Mei:

"Excuse me, doctors. Encik Tan has been asking questions about his treatment options. He seems interested in understanding all possibilities."

Dr. Ahmad:

"Thank you, Sister Mei. I'll speak with him later. For now, let's continue with our rounds."

Dr. Kumar:

"(After ward round, speaking privately to Sister Mei) I'm concerned that we're not considering the best evidence-based approach for Encik Tan."

Sister Mei:

"I understand your concern, Dr. Kumar. This is a challenging situation. What do you think would be the best way forward?"
